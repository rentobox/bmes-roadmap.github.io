BMES@UCF Workshop Timeline

A GitHub Pages-friendly planning site for the three main workshop directions: Biomedical Manufacturing Track, Introduction to Biomedical Engineering Series, and Road to BMES Convention / Professional Readiness.
Draft: 2026–2027

Home Screen

Main idea: start most workshops in September, keep the plan centered on the three strongest ideas, and make each idea feel like a real series or track instead of random one-off events.

Idea 1: Biomedical Manufacturing Track

A weekly OTC-centered track where students build manufacturing confidence through CNC/manual milling exposure, GD&T basics, CAD-to-manufacturing thinking, safety, and a final biomedical-related deliverable.

Weekly OTC Cohort Model Flagship Track

Idea 2: Introduction to Biomedical Engineering Series

A beginner-friendly series that helps students understand what BME is, what areas exist, and how engineering, medicine, health science, computing, and research connect.

Beginner Friendly Educational Interactive

Idea 3: Road to BMES Convention / Professional Readiness

A professional development series where students prepare for the BMES Annual Meeting, symposiums, career fairs, networking, resumes, LinkedIn, and talking to industry or research professionals.

Professional Prep Convention Ready Networking

How This Should Be Read

Placeholders are intentional.
Titles like “Biomedical Manufacturing Track #1” are meant to be flexible until exact rooms, partners, dates, and facilitators are confirmed.
OTC is weekly.
The manufacturing track is separated out as a repeating weekly pathway, not mixed randomly into other workshop ideas.
Fall is the pilot.
Fall should prove which structures work. Spring can repeat, expand, or showcase the strongest ideas.
Document everything.
Attendance, photos, flyers, feedback, partner names, and student deliverables should be captured throughout the year.

High-Level Flow

Semester Phase Focus Reason
Late August Officer feedback, interest forms, workshop committee setup Students are still settling into classes, so this is better for planning than major events.
September Launch the three main ideas This is the best month to build momentum before exams and mid-semester stress.
October Run core series, prepare for BMES convention, continue weekly OTC October has the BMES Annual Meeting and the UCF withdrawal deadline, so planning should be intentional.
November Continue OTC, collect outcomes, do lighter follow-up workshops Thanksgiving and late-semester workload make it better for wrap-up and documentation.
Spring Repeat what worked, build toward BMES@UCF Symposium, document for awards/reporting Spring can be used to strengthen the best Fall ideas and create a clearer showcase moment.
